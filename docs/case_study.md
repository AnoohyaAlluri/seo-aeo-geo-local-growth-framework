# Case Study: SEO, AEO & GEO Website Growth Framework

## Overview

This case study documents a public-safe reconstruction of an SEO, AEO, and GEO website growth framework for a local service-business website.

The project demonstrates how website growth work can be organized into a repeatable system that connects search intent, city-page architecture, answer-first content, FAQ planning, schema recommendations, internal linking, content inventory tracking, and conversion-focused calls to action.

This is not a content-writing project only. It is a marketing technology and growth operations framework for making website visibility more structured, scalable, and measurable.

---

## Business Context

Local service businesses often depend on organic search, local visibility, and high-intent website traffic to generate leads.

However, many websites grow in a fragmented way. Service pages, blog posts, city pages, FAQs, and landing pages may be created separately without a unified structure. This can create problems for users, search engines, and AI-driven answer systems.

The framework in this repository shows how a website can be organized around:

* Search intent
* Local service-area visibility
* Answer-first content
* FAQ coverage
* Structured data
* Internal linking
* Conversion paths
* Content operations tracking

---

## Business Problem

The website growth workflow needed a more systematic structure.

Common problems in local service-business websites include:

* City pages are inconsistent across locations.
* Service pages are not always connected to city pages.
* FAQs are missing, thin, or not aligned with search intent.
* Blog content is not clearly connected to lead-generation pages.
* Internal links are inconsistent.
* CTAs are not always aligned with the visitor’s intent.
* Schema planning is incomplete or not documented.
* AI-answer readiness is not treated as part of the website structure.
* Content priorities are difficult to track without a clear inventory.

The business risk is that strong content may still underperform if it is not connected through a clear architecture.

## SEO Strategy Layer

Key SEO elements include:

* Local service-area page planning
* Keyword-intent mapping
* Page type alignment
* Internal linking
* Content inventory tracking
* Service-page and city-page relationships
* FAQ support

The goal is to make pages easier to crawl, understand, and connect within a larger site structure.

---

## AEO Strategy Layer

The AEO layer focuses on answer-first content.

AEO principles used in this framework include:

* Answer the main question near the top of the page.
* Use clear headings.
* Use concise FAQ answers.
* Align answers with search intent.
* Avoid long introductions before the answer.
* Connect FAQs to relevant service pages.
* Support FAQPage schema where appropriate.

The goal is to make content easier for users and answer systems to extract.

---

## GEO Strategy Layer

The GEO layer focuses on generative engine visibility and AI-readable structure.

GEO principles used in this framework include:

* Make entity relationships clear.
* Connect services, locations, audiences, and problems.
* Use consistent service-area terminology.
* Build structured page templates.
* Include answer-first sections.
* Use FAQ blocks.
* Add schema recommendations.
* Connect related pages through internal links.
* Keep CTAs clear and logical.

The goal is to make content easier for AI-driven systems to understand, summarize, and associate with the correct service and location.

---

## Internal Linking Strategy

The framework uses internal links to connect website sections into a clearer content system.

Recommended structure:

```text
Homepage
      ↓
Core Service Pages
      ↓
Priority City Pages
      ↓
Guides and FAQ Pages
      ↓
Contact or Consultation Pages
```

Internal linking helps:

* Connect research pages to service pages
* Connect city pages to core services
* Support topical authority
* Improve navigation
* Strengthen AI-readable page relationships
* Move users toward conversion actions

## Schema Planning

The framework documents schema examples by page type.

| Page Type | Recommended Schema |
| --------- | ------------------ |
| Homepage | LocalBusiness |
| Service Page | Service |
| City Page | LocalBusiness or Service |
| Guide Page | Article |
| FAQ Page | FAQPage |
| Blog Index | CollectionPage |

Schema is used to clarify the page type and entity relationships. It should match visible page content and should not include unsupported claims.

## Conversion Strategy

Each page type should guide the visitor toward a logical next step.

| Page Type | Recommended CTA |
| --------- | --------------- |
| Service Page | Schedule Consultation |
| City Page | Contact Form or Schedule Consultation |
| Guide Page | Request Analysis |
| FAQ Page | Schedule Consultation |
| Blog Page | Read Related Guide or Contact Form |

The goal is to connect visibility with lead-generation pathways.
